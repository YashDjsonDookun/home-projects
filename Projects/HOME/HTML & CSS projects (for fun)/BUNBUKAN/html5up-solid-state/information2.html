Goju Ryu Styles of Karate

Traditional Karate originates from the island of okinawa. Originally, there existed only 3 styles, each of which was named after the city from which it started. These are: Tomari-te, Shuri-te and Naha-te. The Tomrari-te and Shuri-te were unified to form one school known as Shorin-te while Naha-te remained to its true form and became known as Goju Ryu.

The Meaning of Goju Ryu

Goju Ryu Karate Do techniques can be traced back to the Bubishi. The Bubishi is an ancient martial art text and it is not known where it came from. There is no evidence to suggest that it was written or compiled in Southern China or in Okinawa, but the text was originally written in Chinese and contained notes on White Crane and Monk Fist (Shaolin) Kung Fu arts

The first school in of Naha Te system was opened by "Kanryo Higaonna" during 1870s in Naha Te, Okinawa.

According to oriental philosophy, to achieve harmony and order in the world, everything must be balanced, just as there is night & day and fire & water and so on.

The founder of our style, Grandmaster Chojun Miyagi chose the name Goju Ryo to express the balance in the style. "Go" means hard or resilient; "Ju" means soft or yielding.Therefore, Goju Ryu translates as the hard-soft school. This refers specifically to both technical characteristics of the style and its underlying philosophy.

Foreword

The school is run as a non-profit association affiliated to the Institute of Classical Japanese Budo Culture which was formed by Shihan Chris Rowen after the passing of late Hanshi Gogen Yamaguichi.
