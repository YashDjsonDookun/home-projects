what is the meaning of BUNBUKAN?

It was established for the purpose of protecting and preserving the tradition of Goju Ryu Karate-Do and its original form as passed on by Goju Ryu founder, Choju Miyagi.

Bunbukan was formed as a vehicle for the continuation and preservation of the practice and principles of the art of Karate Do and its spiritual aspects. Its prime philosophy is to promote and disseminate knowledge, so it can be fostered and shared with anyone, who has the same interest, in a non-rigid and non-dogmatic way of learning Goju Ryu Karate Do.

The meaning of Bunbukan lies within three Japanese characters. BUN can be interpreted as mind, study, education, literacy, arts and introspection. BU is the prefix to Budo that represents the way of martial arts; but the deeper meaning gleaned from the ideogram BU is to stop fighting and conflict and it is this aspect that we pursue in Bunbukan. KAN denotes the physical place where the mind and body can be joined in this particular practice.

BUNBUKAN in simple words means: the place where we can study to stop fighting.

  • BUN: The word BUN can be interpreted as "mind, study, education, literacy, arts and introspection"
  • BU: Physical Martial. "Bu is the prefix to Budo, the way of martial arts." Yet, the deeper meaning gleaned from the ideogram BU is to stop fighting and conflict and it is this aspect that we pursue in Bunbukan.
  • KAN: KAN denotes the physical place where the mind and body can be joined in this particular practice.
