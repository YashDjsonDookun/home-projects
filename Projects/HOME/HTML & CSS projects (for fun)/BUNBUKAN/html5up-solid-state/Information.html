What is Karate?

The Japanese term "Kara" means empty and "te" means hand. Therefore, Karate translates as "empty hand", the art of self-defence without a weapon.

Through Bunbukan, you will learn about how to develop mental and physical power. You will learn how to develop tremendous power through correct breathing, coordination of your own body and concentration. You will gain mastery over your body which will help you gain confidence.

Karate is much more than the physical aspect. It is a complete discipline involving 'Kokoro', the heart and the spirit, and karate, the physical body. Through the training of the body, we can discipline the mind and temper the spirit. The most important purpose of karate is to develop balance within ourselves so that we may express our true natue and become better human beings.

Karate for all ages

It promotes self-confidence, it develops coordination and reflex, it builds up strength and courage and most of all, it channels energy to a positive development and self-control. We become strong by training both the mind and the body.

If we are respectful to our parents and pay attention to our teachers, our minds will grow and become strong. We become courageous by being kind to other people and respecting one another's differences in the dojo.

REMEMBER: True warrior or 'Karate-kas' never allow themselves to be provoked into violence. We must always try our best to walk away from any fight. By keeping our karate training for ourselves, we learn self-control.
